Hello! I'm Matt Yang.

I'm a digital archivist and media preservationist based in New York City. My work focuses on the migration of digital audiovisual collections, developing open-source media workflows, preserving software and web-based works, and leveraging web3 technologies for audiovisual preservation.

Currently, I am the Audio Preservation Technician at CUNY TV where I develop audio processing scripts for the Himan Brown Archives. Before this, I worked in digital preservation, film restoration, and media digitization at the Asian Film Archive in Singapore. I completed internships at the Bay Area Video Coalition, Cineric, CUNY TV, the New York Public Library, and Prelinger Archives during my graduate studies in Moving Image Archiving and Preservation (MIAP) at New York University.

Originally from sunny Singapore, I enjoy walking down the aisles of supermarkets in my free time.
