Hello!

I am a digital & media preservationist, and technologist based in Los Angeles. My work focuses on integrating and migrating museum and archival collections, designing open-source data and media workflows for cultural heritage preservation, and preserving web-based works.

Currently, I am the Collection & Content Management Systems Graduate Intern at the J. Paul Getty Trust and the Software Engineering Intern at the Internet Archive. At the Getty, I support its different programs in digital preservation, time-based media conservation, and web archiving, while at the Internet Archive, I develop bespoke tools to perform quality assurance on web archives.

Previously, I worked in digital preservation, film restoration, and media preservation at the Asian Film Archive in Singapore and CUNY TV in New York City. I have also completed internships at the Bay Area Video Coalition (BAVC), Cineric Inc., CUNY TV, the New York Public Library, and Prelinger Archives during my graduate studies in Moving Image Archiving and Preservation (MIAP) at the Tisch School of the Arts, New York University.

Originally from sunny Singapore, I enjoy grocery shopping, sailing, and hiking in my free time.
